I am currently a tenure-track lecturer in finance at Research Center for Commodity Markets and Behaviors, Dongbei University of Finance and Economics (DUFE), China. My research focuses on commodity markets and structural econometric modeling. Currently I am also working on the influences of media coverage in capital markets.

I successfully defended my PhD thesis “Revealing Financial Interactions” in July, 2017 at Maastricht University, the Netherlands. The supervisor is Prof. dr. Jaap W. B. Bos, and the co-supervisor is Dr. Paulo Rodrigues. The preface of my thesis can be found here.

I got my bachelor's degrees in statistics and accounting in 2011 and master's degree in international economics in 2013, all of which are from Wuhan University, P. R. China.

The correct spelling of my name in Roman alphabet (pinyin) is Háng Sūn. Although awkward, it is indeed acceptable to omit the tome marks (diacritics) according to major international standards such as ISO 7098:2015. It is also the de facto standard to romanize Chinese names without the tone marks in western academia. Therefore, for the sake of convenience, I omit the tone marks in academic activities as well.
